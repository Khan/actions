Move the deep-reasoning reviewer roles from Opus 4.8 to Fable 5 (`claude-fable-5`): `correctness-reviewer` and `claim-validator` in the default roster, plus the opt-in whole-change reviewers (`holistic`, `completeness`, `test-adequacy`). Bug-finding recall is the workflow's load-bearing metric and stronger real-defect detection plus adversarial verification are Fable's documented gains over Opus 4.8, so the 2x per-token price goes where it buys measurable review quality. The procedural roles (orchestrator, `thread-reconciler`, `skill-auditor`, `conventions`) stay on Opus 4.8: their work is instruction-following over staged context, and the orchestrator is the token-heaviest agent under a 20-minute timeout. The specialist lenses also stay on Opus 4.8 deliberately; Fable's cyber safety classifiers can refuse benign security-focused analysis, and a refused security lens would be a silent coverage hole. `first-principles` already ran Fable 5; its prompt no longer claims to be the only reviewer on a different model. The firewall pricing pin (awf v0.27.27) and the `models:` cost block already cover `claude-fable-5`, so no sandbox or pricing change is needed.
